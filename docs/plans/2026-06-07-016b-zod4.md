## 4. Decisions

### `.email()` / `.uuid()` â€” MIGRATE to top-level `z.email()` / `z.uuid()`
Rationale: top-level is the v4-recommended, future-proof form (the chained methods are deprecated and slated for removal in a future major; top-level also enables tree-shaking). Behavior is identical â€” same validation, and our tests assert via argument-less `.toThrow()` / success-path property checks, NOT on message text, so the v4 message rewording does not matter. Migrating now avoids leaving deprecated calls that a future `no-deprecated` lint rule or zod 5 would break. This keeps the build/lint warning-free and is the lowest-future-cost option. (Leaving them chained would ALSO build/lint green today, but accrues deprecation debt.)

### `.merge()` â€” MIGRATE to `.extend(...shape)` in `mail.ts`
Rationale: `.merge()` is deprecated in v4; `.extend()` is the recommended replacement AND is already the codebase's dominant idiom (22 existing sites). Two-site change, behavior-identical.

### `.default()` / `.refine()` / object strictness â€” LEAVE AS-IS
No behavior change for our values/usage (see Â§2). Do not redesign.
